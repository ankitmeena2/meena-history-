Research

A curated bibliography of books, journals, colonial gazetteers, government reports and manuscripts relevant to Meena history, political economy and heritage across Rajasthan.

Note on sources: Meena history is documented unevenly across available archives. Colonial gazetteers and revenue records, while produced under British administrative interest, remain the most detailed primary source base for 19th-century Meena territorial and social organization. Community-generated sources — vanshavallis, oral histories, folk genealogies — are underrepresented in formal archives and represent an urgent area for collection and preservation.

Archive
Rajasthan State Archives — Bikaner
Rajasthan State Archives
Bikaner, Rajasthan

The single most important archival repository for Meena historical research. Holdings include British-period revenue settlement reports, court records, village surveys, and Rajput state administrative documents. Researchers must apply for access. The Persian and Hindi language records of the Kachhwaha and other states contain Meena references awaiting systematic study.

Primary ArchiveBikanerIn-Person Access
Manuscript
Vanshavallis (Genealogical Recitations) — Meena Community
Community bards and genealogists (Jagas)
Oral / manuscript tradition, various locations

Meena vanshavallis — genealogical recitations maintained by community bards — are among the most important internally generated sources for Meena history. They contain clan lineages, territorial claims, and historical events not recorded in external sources. Systematic collection and digitization of these traditions is an urgent preservation priority.

The Jaga community serves as hereditary genealogists for Meena clans, maintaining recitations of clan lineages and territorial claims across generations. These traditions provide the most granular internal data on Meena clan organization and history, and are at risk as oral transmission weakens.

Sacred Site Traditions — Kuldevtas and Community Shrines
Community tradition
Living tradition, multiple locations

Meena clan kuldevtas (clan deities) and associated shrines carry territorial and historical memory embedded in religious practice. The geography of these shrines maps onto historical Meena settlement patterns and can be used as one form of evidence for reconstructing territorial presence prior to documentary records.
