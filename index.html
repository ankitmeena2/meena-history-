About This Archive

Preserving a civilization older than recorded history

The Meena people are among the oldest indigenous communities of Rajasthan, with roots stretching to the pre-Vedic era. This archive brings together historical records, genealogies, archaeological sites and scholarly research to document a heritage that colonial and nationalist historiography long overlooked.

Featured Entry

Amber: The Meena Stronghold Before the Rajputs

Before Amber became the legendary seat of the Kachhwaha Rajputs, it was held by the Meena chiefs of the Susawat clan. Historical and folk records suggest that Raja Man Singh's ancestors took control of Amber from the Meena in the 11th–12th centuries CE — a transition that reshaped the political geography of what is now Jaipur district. The Meena role in constructing and maintaining the earlier fort structures is a subject of ongoing historical inquiry.
